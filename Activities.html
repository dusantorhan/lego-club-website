Activities

Our Activities

In our LEGO Robotics Club, students learn through building, programming and teamwork. Each lesson brings new challenges and creative tasks.

Building

Students start with basic constructions and simple motorized models, gradually moving to working with sensors and more complex builds. Advanced students also explore gear systems and basic principles of mechanics.

Programming

Students start with block-based programming and gradually move to Scratch-like environments. Some advanced students also begin working with programming languages such as Python.

Teamwork

Students learn to work as a team following the First LEGO League (FLL) principles. They collaborate, share ideas and support each other while solving challenges. We focus on respect, fair play and learning together.

Problem-Solving

Problem-solving is an important part of our club and the First LEGO League experience. Students learn to identify challenges, discuss possible solutions, test their ideas and improve their work through trial and error.

Competition Success

Older Group (10–16 years)

Our older group participated in the First LEGO League regional competition for the first time. They successfully presented their work and received the “Level Up” award for their progress and development.

Younger Group (6–9 years)

Our younger group also took part in the regional competition in Bratislava and successfully advanced to the national finals in Žilina. They received the “Creative Story” award for their project.

How Our Sessions Work

  • Introduction to the topic
  • Building and experimenting
  • Testing and improving models
  • Discussion and presentation

Why Students Enjoy It

Students enjoy creating their own ideas, working with friends and seeing their projects come to life. Every session is active, creative and fun.
